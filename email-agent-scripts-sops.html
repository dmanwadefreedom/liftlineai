Script F - Post-Meeting Follow-Up (Voice Note to Email)

Post-Meeting

The wholesaler records a quick voice note after each meeting. The AI processes the audio, extracts key details, and generates a personalized follow-up email.

Input - Wholesaler Voice Note
"Just left Mike Chen's office. Great meeting. We talked about his concerns around duration risk in his fixed income sleeve - he's got about 40% in long-duration treasuries. I showed him our short-duration municipal strategy and he was really interested. He wants to see a comparison of our muni fund vs what he's currently using. Also mentioned his daughter is starting at Stanford in the fall, so congrats on that. He's open to a follow-up call in two weeks once he's reviewed the materials."
Output - Generated Email
Subject: Great catching up today - duration analysis to follow

Hi Mike,

Really enjoyed our conversation today. Congratulations on your daughter heading to Stanford this fall - that's a huge accomplishment.

Per our discussion on the duration exposure in your fixed income allocation, I'm putting together a side-by-side comparison of our short-duration municipal strategy against your current positioning. I'll have that over to you by end of week.

Let's plan to reconnect the week of [Date + 2 weeks] once you've had a chance to review. I'll send a calendar invite.

Thanks again for making the time.

Best,
[Wholesaler Name]
AI extracts 4 things from every voice note
1. Personal details to reference (builds relationship)
2. Specific investment topics discussed (shows attentiveness)
3. Action items and next steps (drives follow-through)
4. Suggested follow-up timeline (keeps pipeline moving)

Script G - Rescheduling Handler

Reschedule

When an advisor replies with "can't make it," "need to move this," or similar language, the AI detects the intent and handles rescheduling automatically.

Input - Advisor Reply
"Hey, something came up with a client emergency. Can we push this to later in the week?"
Output - Auto-Generated Response
Hi [Advisor Name],

No problem at all - client always comes first. Let me grab a couple of options for later this week:

- [Day 1] at [Time A] or [Time B]
- [Day 2] at [Time A] or [Time B]

Or if next week works better, [Wholesaler] can do a quick phone call. Whatever's easiest for you.

[Assistant Name]
Rescheduling Rules
The system offers alternatives from the same zone visit window first. If no slots are available in that rotation, it offers a phone call as a bridge. The original calendar slot is freed up immediately and offered to the next advisor in the priority queue. Max 2 reschedule attempts per meeting before it's logged as a cancellation.

Standard Operating Procedures

SOP 1

Zone Rotation Scheduling

How the 5-Zone System Works

Each wholesaler's territory is divided into 5 geographic zones. The wholesaler rotates through one zone per week on a repeating cycle. This creates predictable travel patterns, reduces windshield time, and ensures every part of the territory gets consistent coverage.

Scheduling Window

The active scheduling window is 2 weeks out from the target zone visit. This gives advisors enough notice without being so far ahead that schedules change.

  • Week -2: AI begins outreach to advisors in the target zone (A-tier first)
  • Week -1: Follow-up sequence for non-responders; B and C tier outreach begins
  • Target Week: Meetings execute; last-minute fills from prospect list
  • Week +1: Post-meeting follow-ups sent; data logged in Salesforce

Zone Assignment Logic

Advisors are assigned to zones based on their primary office location. The system uses the office ZIP code from Salesforce and maps it to the nearest zone. Advisors with multiple office locations are assigned to the zone where they spend the most days per week (tracked via Salesforce custom field).

SOP 2

Advisor Prioritization

Tier Definitions

Tier Definition Visit Frequency Scheduling Priority
A Tier Top 20% of book. Highest AUM and revenue. Strong existing relationship. Every rotation (every 5 weeks) Always first. Calendar slots reserved before any other outreach.
B Tier Solid relationships. Consistent but not top-tier production. Every other rotation or on request Second priority. Scheduled after A-tier slots are confirmed.
C Tier Smaller accounts or newer relationships. Growth potential. Quarterly or to fill calendar gaps Third priority. Used to fill open time slots within the zone.
Prospect No existing relationship. Identified through Broadridge data or referrals. 1-2 per week maximum Lowest priority. Never displaces an existing relationship.

Tier Movement Rules

  • Promotion (C to B, B to A): Based on trailing 12-month revenue growth, meeting engagement rate, and wholesaler recommendation. Reviewed quarterly.
  • Demotion (A to B, B to C): Triggered by 2 consecutive rotation no-shows without rescheduling, significant AUM decline, or advisor request for reduced contact.
  • Prospect to C: Automatic after first successful in-person meeting and any fund flow activity within 90 days.

SOP 4

Compliance Protocol

FINRA/SEC Compliance is Non-Negotiable
Every email template must be reviewed and approved by the firm's compliance department before deployment. No exceptions.

Email Channel Requirements

  • All outbound emails must be sent through the firm's Microsoft 365 / Outlook system (FINRA requirement for archival and supervision)
  • No personal email, Gmail, or third-party email tools for advisor communications
  • All emails are automatically captured by the firm's archival system (Smarsh or Global Relay)

Content Restrictions

  • No performance claims, past returns, or projected returns in any email
  • No guarantees or promises about investment outcomes
  • No comparison to competitor products unless pre-approved by compliance
  • No forward-looking statements about market performance
  • Standard firm disclaimer appended to all outgoing messages automatically

Audit Trail

  • Every email sent is logged in Salesforce with: timestamp, template used, advisor tier, zone, and response status
  • Full conversation threads are preserved and searchable
  • Monthly compliance report generated showing all advisor touchpoints
  • Template version history maintained - every edit tracked with approval date

Pre-Deployment Checklist

  • All templates reviewed by compliance team
  • Standard disclaimers configured in Outlook signature
  • Archival system (Smarsh/Global Relay) capturing AI-sent emails
  • No performance data or guarantees in any template
  • Salesforce logging verified for all email events
  • Supervisor review workflow enabled in Outlook

Broadridge FMAX Integration

Broadridge FMAX data enriches advisor records with competitive intelligence:

  • Fund flows: Which fund families are gaining or losing assets with each advisor
  • Competitive intel: Which competitor wholesalers are active with your advisors
  • Product holdings: What the advisor currently owns - enables targeted conversation starters
  • Activity trends: Trading frequency and product category preferences over time

FMAX data syncs to Salesforce nightly and is used by the email agent to select the appropriate conversation hook for each script.

Calendar Sync

  • Confirmed meetings are created as Salesforce Events linked to the Contact and the Zone
  • Outlook calendar entries are created simultaneously via Microsoft Graph API
  • Two-way sync: changes in either system propagate within 5 minutes
  • Meeting outcomes (completed, cancelled, rescheduled, no-show) are logged as Activity records
SOP 6

Exception Handling

Advisor Out of Office (Extended)

  • If an auto-reply is detected, the advisor is paused from the current follow-up sequence
  • The return date (if included in the auto-reply) is logged in Salesforce
  • The advisor is re-queued for the next zone rotation after their return date
  • If no return date is detected, the advisor is flagged for manual review

Advisor Requests No Further Contact

  • Immediately cease all automated outreach
  • Update Salesforce: set Do_Not_Contact__c to TRUE
  • Notify the wholesaler directly - this requires a personal decision on how to handle
  • The advisor is excluded from all future automated scheduling until the flag is manually removed

Last-Minute Cancellation (Under 2 Hours)

  • System checks for C-tier advisors or prospects in the same geographic cluster
  • If a replacement is available, send an expedited outreach: "Had an opening this afternoon - any chance you're free?"
  • If no replacement is available, log the slot as "cancelled - unfilled" for reporting
  • Original advisor is offered first available slot in the next rotation

Double-Booking Conflicts

  • System checks for overlapping time + travel time between meetings
  • If a conflict is detected, the lower-tier advisor is offered an alternative time
  • If both advisors are the same tier, the one confirmed first keeps the slot
  • Wholesaler is notified of all conflict resolutions with the option to override

Wholesaler Sick or Traveling - Zone Pause

  • Wholesaler (or ops manager) triggers a zone pause via a single command or Salesforce status change
  • All pending outreach for the target zone is held (not deleted)
  • Confirmed meetings are sent a rescheduling email: "Unfortunately, [Wholesaler] needs to reschedule due to an unexpected conflict..."
  • When the pause is lifted, the system resumes from where it left off with updated dates

Section 4

Implementation Timeline

Phased rollout over 7+ weeks. Each phase builds on the previous one. No phase is skipped. Compliance approval is a gate at the end of Week 2 - nothing goes live without it.

Week 1

Foundation - Salesforce Setup

Configure custom fields (Zone, Tier, Last Contact, Preferred Meeting Type, Office Location). Import advisor data. Assign zones based on office ZIP codes. Set initial tier classifications using trailing 12-month revenue data. Validate Broadridge FMAX data feed.

Week 2

Templates + Compliance

Finalize all 7 email scripts (A through G). Submit to compliance for review. Configure Outlook integration via Microsoft Graph API. Set up email archival verification with Smarsh/Global Relay. Configure standard disclaimers. Get written compliance sign-off before proceeding.

Week 3

Pilot Zone Launch

Activate the system for one zone in one territory. A-tier advisors only for the first rotation. Monitor every email sent. Verify Salesforce logging, calendar sync, and archival. Daily check-ins with the wholesaler to gather feedback on tone and timing.

Week 4

Voice Confirmation Activation

Enable VAPI voice confirmation calls for the pilot zone. Test call flow: confirmation script, rescheduling handling, advisor intent detection. Verify callback data syncs to Salesforce and calendar. Fine-tune voice agent persona (professional, warm, not robotic).

Week 5-6

Measure + Adjust

Analyze pilot results: response rate, show rate, advisor feedback, compliance issues (if any). Adjust email copy, follow-up timing, and geographic clustering logic. Expand to B and C tier advisors in the pilot zone. Begin prospect outreach. Generate first weekly and monthly reports.

Week 7+

Multi-Territory Rollout

Roll out to additional territories one at a time. Each new territory follows a compressed 2-week onboarding (data import + first zone activation). Scale to full 5-zone rotation per territory. Begin quarterly ROI reporting. Evaluate adding additional wholesalers to the system.

Success Criteria for Go/No-Go at Each Phase
Week 3 (Pilot): 80%+ of emails delivered successfully, zero compliance flags, Salesforce logging accurate.
Week 4 (Voice): Voice calls completing without errors, 90%+ intent classification accuracy.
Week 5-6 (Measure): Response rate above 25%, show rate above 75%, positive wholesaler feedback.
Week 7+ (Scale): System stable with 2+ territories, reporting automated, no manual intervention needed for standard operations.
